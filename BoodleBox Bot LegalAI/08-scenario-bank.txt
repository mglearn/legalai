# Scenario Bank — 50 Fictional Texas Criminal Defense Fact Patterns

Fifty short, fictional practice scenarios for the LegalAI bot, spanning common Texas criminal defense matters. Each names a charge with its Texas statute, a one or two sentence fact pattern, and a defense focus.

**Use safely.** Every scenario is fictional and simplified for practice. Statute section numbers are provided for orientation only; confirm the current text and elements at statutes.capitol.texas.gov before relying on them. Never replace these with real client-identifying facts in a consumer tool. When a user picks one, apply the TCDLAi framework, ground only in sources the user pastes, and mark anything unsupported.

Client names are single fictional initials. "SBI" means serious bodily injury. Weights, priors, and lab results are invented.

---

## A. DWI and intoxication offenses

1. **No-refusal blood draw** — DWI (Tex. Penal Code § 49.04). Client "R." refused a breath test at a no-refusal weekend; blood was drawn on a warrant. Focus: warrant-affidavit sufficiency, blood-draw procedure, and chain of custody.
2. **Rising alcohol** — DWI (§ 49.04). Breath test of 0.09 taken ninety minutes after driving, right after a meal. Focus: retrograde extrapolation and the rising-BAC defense.
3. **Prescription-medication DWI** — DWI by drugs (§ 49.04). Client "M." took a prescribed medication, no alcohol; the officer alleges impairment. Focus: the statutory definition of intoxication and DRE reliability.
4. **DWI with a child passenger** — (§ 49.045). A fictional eight-year-old was in the back seat. Focus: the state-jail-felony element, the child-passenger fact, and the validity of the stop.
5. **Felony DWI, third** — (§ 49.09). Two fictional prior DWIs are alleged for enhancement. Focus: proving prior convictions and admonishment defects in the priors.
6. **Intoxication assault** — (§ 49.07). A collision caused SBI; causation is disputed. Focus: causation, the SBI definition, and accident reconstruction.
7. **Boating while intoxicated** — (§ 49.06). Client operated a boat on a lake. Focus: reasonable suspicion on the water and field-sobriety validity performed on a dock.
8. **DWI with lost video** — (§ 49.04). The dashcam malfunctioned; only the officer's narrative remains. Focus: reasonable suspicion for the stop, credibility, and the missing recording.

## B. Drug offenses

9. **THC vape cartridge** — Possession, Penalty Group 2 (Tex. Health and Safety Code § 481.116). A cartridge tested positive for Delta-9 THC. Focus: the hemp defense, THC-concentration thresholds, and lab methodology.
10. **THC edibles** — Possession PG2 (§ 481.116). Gummies whose total weight includes non-drug ingredients. Focus: aggregate weight, adulterants and dilutants, and penalty-group classification.
11. **Shared-apartment possession** — Possession PG1 (§ 481.115). A substance was found in the common area of a shared apartment. Focus: affirmative links and joint possession.
12. **Substance in a borrowed trunk** — Possession PG1 (§ 481.115). Drugs were in the trunk of a borrowed car. Focus: knowledge, affirmative links, and ownership.
13. **Drug-free-zone enhancement** — (§ 481.134). Alleged possession within a school zone. Focus: how the distance was measured and proof of the zone element.
14. **Possession with intent to deliver** — PWID PG1 (§ 481.112). Baggies and a scale are cited as intent. Focus: the intent inference, expert testimony, and the personal-use argument.
15. **Paraphernalia with residue** — (§ 481.125). A pipe with residue only. Focus: usable-amount questions and the Class C posture.
16. **Fraudulent prescription** — Fraud to obtain a controlled substance (§ 481.129). An altered prescription is alleged. Focus: knowledge and intent, and pharmacy records.

## C. Search and seizure and procedure

17. **Prolonged stop for a dog sniff** — Possession after a stop (§ 481.115; Fourth Amendment; Tex. Code Crim. Proc. art. 38.23). The stop continued after the warning was written. Focus: unlawful prolongation and suppression.
18. **Search beyond consent** — Client consented to a search of "the car"; an officer opened a locked container. Focus: the scope of consent and suppression.
19. **Warrantless home entry** — Officers entered a residence citing exigency. Focus: exigent circumstances and the limits of community caretaking.
20. **Anonymous-tip stop** — A stop rested solely on an anonymous 911 tip. Focus: reliability, corroboration, and reasonable suspicion.
21. **Inventory-search pretext** — A vehicle was impounded and searched; policy compliance is disputed. Focus: the inventory exception and the standardized-policy requirement.
22. **Custodial statement** — Client was questioned at the station without warnings. Focus: the custody analysis, voluntariness, and article 38.22.
23. **Warrant particularity** — A search warrant described the wrong unit number. Focus: particularity, good-faith limits under Texas law, and suppression.

## D. Assaultive offenses and family violence

24. **Assault, family violence** — (Tex. Penal Code § 22.01(b)). Mutual-combat facts; the complainant recants. Focus: self-defense, recantation, and the 911 call as an excited utterance.
25. **Violation of a protective order** — (§ 25.07). Contact is alleged through a third party. Focus: knowledge of the order and the contact element.
26. **Continuous family violence** — (§ 25.11). Two incidents within twelve months are alleged. Focus: the two-incident requirement and jury unanimity.
27. **Aggravated assault, SBI** — (§ 22.02). SBI is disputed after a bar fight. Focus: the SBI definition, self-defense, and provocation.
28. **Aggravated assault, deadly weapon** — (§ 22.02(a)(2)). A common object is alleged to be a deadly weapon. Focus: the deadly-weapon definition and manner of use.
29. **Terroristic threat** — (§ 22.07). Text messages are alleged as threats. Focus: intent to place in fear, context, and speech limits.
30. **Deadly conduct** — (§ 22.05). A firearm was discharged toward a habitation. Focus: recklessness, identity, and knowledge that someone was present.
31. **Injury to a child** — (§ 22.04). A caregiver is accused after an accidental injury. Focus: the mental state, causation, and the reasonable-discipline question.

## E. Property and theft

32. **Aggregated shoplifting** — Theft (§ 31.03). Several small thefts are aggregated to a felony. Focus: the aggregation rules, value, and the one-scheme requirement.
33. **Theft by check** — (§ 31.03 with § 31.06). A dishonored check; intent to deprive is disputed. Focus: rebutting the statutory presumption and notice.
34. **Unauthorized use of a vehicle** — (§ 31.07). Client drove a relative's car; consent is disputed. Focus: effective consent and mistake of fact.
35. **Burglary of a habitation** — (§ 30.02). Entry into a detached garage. Focus: habitation versus building and intent at entry.
36. **Burglary of a vehicle** — (§ 30.04). Items were taken from an unlocked truck bed. Focus: the vehicle definition, the entry element, and identity.
37. **Criminal mischief** — (§ 28.03). The damage valuation is disputed. Focus: pecuniary loss, intent, and identity.
38. **Robbery** — (§ 29.02). A shoplifting turned into an alleged shove on the way out. Focus: the force element and whether it was in the course of theft.

## F. Weapons

39. **Unlawful carrying of a weapon** — (Tex. Penal Code § 46.02). A handgun in a vehicle, with post-HB 1927 carry nuances. Focus: lawful-carry exceptions and prohibited-person status.
40. **Felon in possession of a firearm** — (§ 46.04). A firearm in a shared home; access is disputed. Focus: possession and affirmative links, and the statutory time window.
41. **Places weapons prohibited** — (§ 46.03). A firearm is alleged at a prohibited location. Focus: notice, knowledge, and the location element.
42. **Prohibited weapon** — (§ 46.05). An alleged short-barreled firearm. Focus: the definition, federal compliance, and knowledge.

## G. Homicide and serious violence

43. **Manslaughter, single-vehicle crash** — (§ 19.04). A passenger died; recklessness is disputed. Focus: recklessness versus criminal negligence and causation.
44. **Murder and self-defense** — (§ 19.02; §§ 9.31, 9.32). A confrontation at the client's home. Focus: self-defense and defense of habitation, and reasonableness.
45. **Criminally negligent homicide** — (§ 19.05). A firearm discharged while being cleaned. Focus: the negligence standard and accident.

## H. Public order, evading, and obstruction

46. **Evading arrest with a vehicle** — (§ 38.04). Client says he did not know it was police. Focus: knowledge and the lawfulness of the attempted detention.
47. **Resisting arrest** — (§ 38.03). Alleged pulling away during handcuffing. Focus: the use-of-force element and the lawfulness of the arrest.
48. **Failure to identify** — (§ 38.02). A false name given during a stop. Focus: arrest versus detention and the elements.
49. **Tampering with evidence** — (§ 37.09). A baggie allegedly discarded during a stop. Focus: knowledge of an investigation and the concealment element.
50. **Online solicitation of a minor** — (§ 33.021). A sting where the "minor" was an officer. Focus: intent, the belief element, and entrapment.
