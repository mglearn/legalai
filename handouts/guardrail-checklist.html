Ethical Guardrail Checklist

Run this before you rely on any AI-assisted work
If any box is unchecked, the work is not ready to leave your desk

The eight-point check

  • Confidentiality. Kept client-identifying facts out of the tool
  • Competence. I understand this tool's limits for this task
  • Grounding. The model worked from sources I trust
  • Verification. I opened and confirmed every citation myself
  • Judgment. I made the strategic calls, not the model
  • Candor. Everything I will file is actually true
  • Supervision. I reviewed this like a new associate's draft
  • Record. I can explain later how this work was produced
The duties behind the check
DutyTexas ruleWhat it means for AI use
Competence and diligence1.01Understand the tool well enough to use it, or do not use it.
Confidentiality1.05Client information stays protected, including from the tools you type it into.
Supervision of nonlawyers5.01, 5.03Treat AI as an assistant whose work you must check. You own the result.
Candor to the tribunal3.03What you file must be true because you confirmed it.

Rule numbers reference the Texas Disciplinary Rules of Professional Conduct and are illustrative. Confirm the current text before relying on them. Nothing here is legal advice.

Do and do not

✓ Do

  • Use AI to draft, summarize, outline, and brainstorm angles
  • Ground it in statutes, opinions, and your own materials
  • Verify every citation in a real database before you cite it
  • Use fictional or de-identified facts in consumer tools
  • Keep the final judgment, and the signature, yours

✗ Do not

  • File any citation you have not personally read
  • Paste privileged or client-identifying facts into consumer tools
  • Treat a confident answer as a verified one
  • Let the model choose strategy or make the client's call
  • Assume grounding or a clean format removes your duty to check
